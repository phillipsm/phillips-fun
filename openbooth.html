OpenBooth Case Study

In this case study we'll step through the process used to define the form and features of OpenBooth.

This documents efforts from 2017 to 2019 in Public Good Studio in Menlo Park, California.

Get a booth at OpenBooth.store.

Context

OpenBooth is a phone booth. A piece of occupiable furniture that creates an aural decoupling from surrounding chatter.

OpenBooth grew out of a project I started in 2016 at the Harvard Library Innovation Lab.

In 2018, I founded Public Good Studio to fabricate and sell OpenBooths. The shop and design studio is currently in operation in Menlo Park, California.

Enter any URL and Perma will generate a web archive for you.

Problem

Open offices, libraries and similar working environments often lack single occupancy rooms that support phone calls.

In addition, the general construction costs associated with adding booths is prohibitive for many organizations.

We no longer need phones in phonebooths, but we still need the privacy afforded by the booth.

Solution

The spaces for phone calls should be more like furniture than a permanent room - we want to avoid permitting, fire code concerns and construction expenses.

The result is a piece of occupiable furniture that can be shipped and relocated on site.

Gather similar ideas and approaches. Push for new possibilities.

Output will be a set of inspirational photos and mockups of rough shapes.

Step four

Refine and focus. Validate.

Output will consist of one or two medium-fidelity wireframes.

Once we have a feel for our object, we can begin the great translation. I transcribed paper sketches into 3D CAD parts and grouped those parts into five subassemblies.

Step five

Documentaiton, prototyping and handoff.

Output will consist of interactive prototypes and feature documentation.

Our primary goal as we wrap up is to support the handoff to the next team by providing detailed notes and passing along critical user requests.

It's important to keep the energy strong during the handoff - we want to catch last minute opportunities and spot pitfalls to be avoided.

Finished

As we ship this feature, let's grab another task in the queue. In the case of Perma, we found that once users could generate and view web archives, they wanted to organize the pile of links they'd created.

So, another journey starts with step one and the question of, "what type of admin interface will best support the user as they organize their archives?"

Tools

I used SolidWorks 2018 to generate CAD models, and Autodesk's HSMWorks to generate toolpaths in CAM. I fabricated booths with a 3 axis CNC router controlled by LinuxCNC.

Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop were used to generate documentation. GitHub and Jekyll power this portfolio site.
